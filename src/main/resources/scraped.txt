<p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Touhou_Project" title="Touhou Project">Touhou Project</a></i> features a great number and variety of <b>characters</b>. Over the course of 30 games, literature, and music CDs, the cast has grown to having over 180 characters. <a href="/wiki/Reimu_Hakurei" title="Reimu Hakurei">Reimu Hakurei</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marisa_Kirisame" title="Marisa Kirisame">Marisa Kirisame</a> are considered the protagonists of the <i>Touhou Project</i>, due to being the most frequent playable characters in the games. Others characters' roles in the games consist of supporting characters, <a href="/wiki/Boss" title="Boss">bosses</a>, or minor appearances. However, due to a diverging points in the series, some of the characters (namely, the majority of the <a href="/wiki/PC-98" title="PC-98">PC-98</a> ones) have not been mentioned again.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="General_Information">General Information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/index.php?title=Characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: General Information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With its focus on <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/bish%C5%8Djo" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:bishōjo">bishoujo</a> characters, the Touhou series possesses a large cast compared to other <a href="/wiki/Shoot_%27em_up" class="mw-redirect" title="Shoot &#39;em up">shoot 'em up</a> games, maybe even those of <a href="/wiki/Shooting_game" title="Shooting game">shooting games</a>, and are notoriously female. Most Touhou characters reside in a fictional landlocked region called <a href="/wiki/Gensokyo" title="Gensokyo">Gensokyo</a>, where <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">humans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Youkai" title="Youkai">youkai</a> live. The youkai often prey on the humans while the humans try to exterminate them. A character's most common role in a <i><a href="/wiki/Touhou_Project" title="Touhou Project">Touhou Project</a></i> game is being a <a href="/wiki/Boss" title="Boss">boss</a>, and they have <a href="/wiki/Characters/Titles" title="Characters/Titles">titles</a> and a theme song associated with them. Midbosses, however, tend to not have a unique theme. Many characters also have unique <a href="/wiki/Characters/Abilities" title="Characters/Abilities">abilities</a>, and almost all <a href="/wiki/Windows" title="Windows">Windows</a> characters have at least one ability named in the fashion of "capable of ~~"<span style="font-weight: normal">&#32;(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">~~程度の能力</span>)</span>, showing their strength and power with various capabilities.
</p><p>Though each game features a collection of different characters, the main character of the games is always <a href="/wiki/Reimu_Hakurei" title="Reimu Hakurei">Reimu Hakurei</a>, joined by <a href="/wiki/Marisa_Kirisame" title="Marisa Kirisame">Marisa Kirisame</a>. The only games where this is not the case are <i><a href="/wiki/Shoot_the_Bullet" title="Shoot the Bullet">Shoot the Bullet</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Double_Spoiler" title="Double Spoiler">Double Spoiler</a></i>; <a href="/wiki/Aya_Shameimaru" title="Aya Shameimaru">Aya Shameimaru</a> is playable on both, and <a href="/wiki/Hatate_Himekaidou" title="Hatate Himekaidou">Hatate Himekaidou</a> can become playable in the latter. The same thing applies in <i><a href="/wiki/Fairy_Wars" class="mw-redirect" title="Fairy Wars">Fairy Wars</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Impossible_Spell_Card" title="Impossible Spell Card">Impossible Spell Card</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Violet_Detector" title="Violet Detector">Violet Detector</a></i> which has <a href="/wiki/Cirno" title="Cirno">Cirno</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seija_Kijin" title="Seija Kijin">Seija Kijin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sumireko_Usami" title="Sumireko Usami">Sumireko Usami</a> as the sole playable character in their respective games.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="PC-98_Games">PC-98 Games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/index.php?title=Characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: PC-98 Games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Th03cover.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="/images/thumb/1/15/Th03cover.jpg/150px-Th03cover.jpg" width="150" height="218" class="thumbimage" srcset="/images/thumb/1/15/Th03cover.jpg/225px-Th03cover.jpg 1.5x, /images/1/15/Th03cover.jpg 2x" /></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Th03cover.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Cover of <i><a href="/wiki/Phantasmagoria_of_Dim.Dream" title="Phantasmagoria of Dim.Dream">Phantasmagoria of Dim.Dream</a></i>, featuring <a href="/wiki/PC-98" title="PC-98">PC-98</a> characters that appear in the game.</div></div></div>
<p>As of <i><a href="/wiki/Embodiment_of_Scarlet_Devil" title="Embodiment of Scarlet Devil">Embodiment of Scarlet Devil</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/ZUN" title="ZUN">ZUN</a> decided to develop games for the <a href="/wiki/Windows" title="Windows">Windows</a> platform and flesh out the universe starting from a clean slate. Besides the <a href="/wiki/Category:Returning_PC-98_Characters" title="Category:Returning PC-98 Characters">Returning characters from the PC-98</a>, none of the other characters from the PC-98 games have reappeared in the Windows era, although cases such as <a href="/wiki/Mima" title="Mima">Mima</a>'s appearance on the case of <i><a href="/wiki/Akyu%27s_Untouched_Score_vol.4" title="Akyu&#39;s Untouched Score vol.4">Akyu's Untouched Score vol.4</a></i> exist.
</p><p>PC-98 characters tend to have a title and music theme, but no ability has being explicitly stated to most characters. They also don't have articles and interviews, or other appearances in print works.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Windows_Games">Windows Games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/index.php?title=Characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Windows Games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Another minor pivotal point exists with the release of <i><a href="/wiki/Mountain_of_Faith" title="Mountain of Faith">Mountain of Faith</a></i>, marking the "2nd Windows generation". While previous games had independent storylines, the plots of <i>Mountain of Faith</i> up to <i><a href="/wiki/Ten_Desires" title="Ten Desires">Ten Desires</a></i> were connected (e.g. The events of <i>Ten Desires</i> were triggered by the construction of the <a href="/wiki/Myouren_Temple" title="Myouren Temple">Myouren Temple</a>, which in turn was triggered by the Palanquin ship being released by the geyser by <a href="/wiki/Utsuho_Reiuji" title="Utsuho Reiuji">Utsuho Reiuji</a>'s power.) However, <i><a href="/wiki/Double_Dealing_Character" title="Double Dealing Character">Double Dealing Character</a></i> contains no storyline related to the previous games, so it could mark another minor pivotal point for a possible "3rd Windows generation".
</p><p>The character appearances trend also resets. For example, <a href="/wiki/Sakuya_Izayoi" title="Sakuya Izayoi">Sakuya Izayoi</a> was a stage 5 boss in <i>Embodiment of Scarlet Devil</i> who later became playable in <i><a href="/wiki/Perfect_Cherry_Blossom" title="Perfect Cherry Blossom">Perfect Cherry Blossom</a></i> up to <i><a href="/wiki/Phantasmagoria_of_Flower_View" title="Phantasmagoria of Flower View">Phantasmagoria of Flower View</a></i>, but has not made an appearance after that in any danmaku Touhou game up until <i><a href="/wiki/Double_Dealing_Character" title="Double Dealing Character">Double Dealing Character</a></i>.
</p>
<ul><li><b>Fighting Games</b></li></ul>
<p>A consistent cast can be seen in the fighting games. Rather than inventing a new set of characters, only one or two new characters were added with the rest coming from previous games. So far, only four characters have first appeared in a fighting game.
</p>
<ul><li><b>Photography Games &amp; Puzzles</b></li></ul>
<p>In <i><a href="/wiki/Shoot_the_Bullet" title="Shoot the Bullet">Shoot the Bullet</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Double_Spoiler" title="Double Spoiler">Double Spoiler</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Impossible_Spell_Card" title="Impossible Spell Card">Impossible Spell Card</a></i>, characters from previous games are used. <i>Shoot the Bullet</i> contains most characters from <i>Embodiment of Scarlet Devil</i> to <i>Phantasmagoria of Flower View</i>, while <i>Double Spoiler</i> adds the cast from <i>Mountain of Faith</i> up to <i><a href="/wiki/Undefined_Fantastic_Object" title="Undefined Fantastic Object">Undefined Fantastic Object</a></i>. <i>Impossible Spell Card</i> contained a variety of characters, mostly from <i><a href="/wiki/Ten_Desires" title="Ten Desires">Ten Desires</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Double_Dealing_Character" title="Double Dealing Character">Double Dealing Character</a></i>.
</p>
<ul><li><b>Literature</b></li></ul>
<p>The cast of Touhou characters also grows with the addition of the novels and comics. Only <a href="/wiki/Aya_Shameimaru" title="Aya Shameimaru">Aya Shameimaru</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Three_Fairies_Of_Light" class="mw-redirect" title="Three Fairies Of Light">Three Fairies Of Light</a> were later featured in a game. Touhou literature is directly connected with the Windows games, as events that happen within the literature are mentioned in the Windows games, and vice versa. <i><a href="/wiki/Perfect_Memento_in_Strict_Sense" title="Perfect Memento in Strict Sense">Perfect Memento in Strict Sense</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Symposium_of_Post-mysticism" title="Symposium of Post-mysticism">Symposium of Post-mysticism</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Bohemian_Archive_in_Japanese_Red" title="Bohemian Archive in Japanese Red">Bohemian Archive in Japanese Red</a></i> have in-universe articles and interviews about individual characters, along with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Grimoire_of_Marisa" title="The Grimoire of Marisa">The Grimoire of Marisa</a></i> having <a href="/wiki/Spell_card" title="Spell card">spell cards</a> related to the characters.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Seihou_Games">Seihou Games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/index.php?title=Characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Seihou Games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kioh_Gyoku.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="/images/thumb/f/fd/Kioh_Gyoku.jpg/150px-Kioh_Gyoku.jpg" width="150" height="151" class="thumbimage" srcset="/images/thumb/f/fd/Kioh_Gyoku.jpg/225px-Kioh_Gyoku.jpg 1.5x, /images/thumb/f/fd/Kioh_Gyoku.jpg/300px-Kioh_Gyoku.jpg 2x" /></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kioh_Gyoku.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Cover of <i><a href="/wiki/Kioh_Gyoku" title="Kioh Gyoku">Kioh Gyoku</a></i>, featuring Seihou characters that appear in the game.</div></div></div>
<dl><dd><i><b>Note:</b> This section is part of the <a href="/wiki/Seihou_Project" title="Seihou Project">Seihou Project</a> by the Doujin circle "<a href="/wiki/Shunsatsu_sare_do%3F" title="Shunsatsu sare do?">Shunsatsu sare do?</a>".</i></dd></dl>
<p>Characters of the <i><a href="/wiki/Seihou_Project" title="Seihou Project">Seihou Project</a></i> were created by "<a href="/wiki/Shunsatsu_sare_do%3F" title="Shunsatsu sare do?">Shunsatsu sare do?</a>" and have nothing to do with the <i><a href="/wiki/Touhou_Project" title="Touhou Project">Touhou Project</a></i>, although they also do have their own theme music. The <i>Seihou Project</i> however contained characters from the <i>Touhou Project</i>, named <a href="/wiki/Reimu_Hakurei" title="Reimu Hakurei">Reimu Hakurei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marisa_Kirisame" title="Marisa Kirisame">Marisa Kirisame</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yuuka_Kazami" title="Yuuka Kazami">Yuuka Kazami</a>. Even in Touhou, references to Seihou characters are extremely rare, with one coming from <i><a href="/wiki/Dolls_in_Pseudo_Paradise" title="Dolls in Pseudo Paradise">Dolls in Pseudo Paradise</a></i>.
</p><p>It appears that each game contains most characters from the previous. Unlike Touhou characters, Seihou characters are designed to be very Western, which is the opposite of Touhou; even their names are western. They also have more frequent male characters that appear in the <a href="/wiki/Shoot_%27em_up" class="mw-redirect" title="Shoot &#39;em up">shoot 'em ups</a>. Since Seihou is set in the future, the characters are also designed to have a futuristic feeling with then, along with having their own aircraft. However, unlike Touhou characters, most character don't have any <a href="/wiki/Characters/Titles" title="Characters/Titles">titles</a> or <a href="/wiki/Characters/Abilities" title="Characters/Abilities">abilities</a> associated with them and most characters' species are <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">human</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Character_Designs">Character Designs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/index.php?title=Characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Character Designs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:258px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Th14RaikoIntroduction.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="/images/f/ff/Th14RaikoIntroduction.png" width="256" height="128" class="thumbimage" /></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Th14RaikoIntroduction.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An example of in-game introduction to a character's name (namely <a href="/wiki/Raiko_Horikawa" title="Raiko Horikawa">Raiko Horikawa</a>): top, title; middle, character's name in Japanese; bottom, the romaji of the name in Japanese naming order.</div></div></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Names">Names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/index.php?title=Characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Generally, almost all names for each character are written in the Japanese script, either in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hiragana" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Hiragana">hiragana</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Katakana" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Katakana">katakana</a> or <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kanji" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Kanji">kanji</a>. Names generally start with <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_name" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Japanese name">the surname followed by the first name</a>, but when they are written in katakana to show a western name, they'll be in western naming order, where they'll have a middle dot (<span lang="ja">・</span>) to show where the surname begins. The names may not truly be actual names in real life, as they may contain kanji to represent the character overall, for example <i>Yuyuko</i><span style="font-weight: normal">&#32;(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">幽々子</span>)</span> literally translates to "ghostly child".
</p><p><a href="/wiki/ZUN" title="ZUN">ZUN</a> has provided <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romanization_of_Japanese" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Romanization of Japanese">romaji</a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kana" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Kana">kana</a> versions for most of the <a href="/wiki/Windows" title="Windows">Windows</a> characters; the latter only included if the name's in kanji. On some rare occurrences, he'd include official English translations of the name. There have been instances in error of romaji however, such as those listed <a href="/wiki/Characters/Spelling_discrepancies" title="Characters/Spelling discrepancies">here</a>. Some characters only have one name, notably the <a href="/wiki/PC-98" title="PC-98">PC-98</a> characters, such as <a href="/wiki/Rika" title="Rika">Rika</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shinki" title="Shinki">Shinki</a>. There are even those that have no name, such as <a href="/wiki/Tokiko" title="Tokiko">Tokiko</a>.
</p><p>For western languages, such as English or French, names are obviously written in the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin_alphabets" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Latin alphabets">Latin script</a>, called <i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romanization" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Romanization">romanization/romanisation</a></i>. Also, the naming order from Japanese is switched to fit suitably with Westerners, for example "Saigyouji Yuyuko"<span style="font-weight: normal">&#32;(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">西行寺　幽々子</span>)</span> becomes "<a href="/wiki/Yuyuko_Saigyouji" title="Yuyuko Saigyouji">Yuyuko Saigyouji</a>". Unofficial romaji may be used for convenience; for example, "Byakuren Hiziri" was officially used, but "<a href="/wiki/Byakuren_Hijiri" title="Byakuren Hijiri">Byakuren Hijiri</a>" has not being officially used yet. Choices and alternative spelling can come in many when romanising katakana; one example is <a href="/wiki/Maribel_Hearn" title="Maribel Hearn">Maribel Hearn</a>, who <a href="/wiki/ZUN" title="ZUN">ZUN</a> doesn't even know the official translation<sup id="cite_ref-ZUN.27s_AWA_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ZUN.27s_AWA-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and has other possible translations such as "Maribel Han" and "Mæriberie Hearn".
</p><p>Eastern languages, namely Chinese and Korean, also have some forms of conversions. Chinese conversion from kanji to <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_characters" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Chinese characters">hanzi</a> depends on the variant of the Chinese language whether it accepts the hanzi/kanji or not; they can sometimes keep their original kanji, but must always be transliterated from hiragana or katakana. Names are always transliterated to <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hangul" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Hangul">hangeul</a> within the Korean language. In cases like Yuyuko's name, since the Japanese iteration mark "<span lang="ja"><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E3%80%85" class="extiw" title="wiktionary:々">々</a></span>", casually called a <i>noma</i><span style="font-weight: normal">&#32;(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ノマ</span>)</span>, isn't formally used in the Chinese or Korean language, it's best to re-use the previous hanzi/hangeul (e.g. <span lang="zh">幽幽子</span>/<span lang="ko">유유코</span>).
</p><p>Fans of both of the western and eastern fanbase have given alternative fan-names for the characters based on their characteristics, such as the <a href="/wiki/Five_Magic_Stones" title="Five Magic Stones">Five Magic Stones</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Designs">Designs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/index.php?title=Characters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Designs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Despite there being many species of <a href="/wiki/Youkai" title="Youkai">youkai</a>, almost all the characters take a <a href="/wiki/Humanoid" title="Humanoid">humanoid</a> form, and are notoriously female. Their designs are notoriously colourful and are based around their characteristics, culture and appearance in a game or manga. It's also notorious that almost all characters have some kind of head wear, such as ribbons or non-human attributes, and that they tend to have a skirt. For characters to not have these attributes is somewhat rare, such as <a href="/wiki/Yuuka_Kazami" title="Yuuka Kazami">Yuuka Kazami</a> not having a hat and <a href="/wiki/Fujiwara_no_Mokou" title="Fujiwara no Mokou">Fujiwara no Mokou</a> wearing trousers respectively.
</p><p>The designs are based on the species a certain character is, such as <a href="/wiki/Nitori_Kawashiro" title="Nitori Kawashiro">Nitori Kawashiro</a> being a <a href="/wiki/Kappa" title="Kappa">kappa</a> and having a backpack to represent a tortoise's shell, and <a href="/wiki/Raiko_Horikawa" title="Raiko Horikawa">Raiko Horikawa</a> having a drum since she is a <a href="/wiki/Tsukumogami" title="Tsukumogami">tsukumogami</a>. This also relates to animal-based characters, such as <a href="/wiki/Chen" title="Chen">Chen</a> having cat ears, <a href="/wiki/Ran_Yakumo" title="Ran Yakumo">Ran Yakumo</a> having nine tails due to being a <a href="/wiki/Kitsune" title="Kitsune">Kitsune</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mamizou_Futatsuiwa" title="Mamizou Futatsuiwa">Mamizou Futatsuiwa</a> having a large tail due to being a <a href="/wiki/Bake-danuki" class="mw-redirect" title="Bake-danuki">bake-danuki</a>.
</p><p>Some characters however have not even been given an official portrait, such as <a href="/wiki/Layla_Prismriver" title="Layla Prismriver">Layla Prismriver</a> and <a href="/wiki/Youki_Konpaku" title="Youki Konpaku">Youki Konpaku</a>. This leads to fans theorising their appearances.
</p>
<dl><dt>Male characters</dt></dl>
<p>It's seldom to see <a href="/wiki/Category:Males" title="Category:Males">male characters</a> appear in the <i><a href="/wiki/Touhou_Project" title="Touhou Project">Touhou Project</a></i> as they are either not named (Various <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">humans</a> shown in <i><a href="/wiki/Hopeless_Masquerade" title="Hopeless Masquerade">Hopeless Masquerade</a></i>), not <a href="/wiki/Humanoid" title="Humanoid">humanoid</a> (<a href="/wiki/Genjii" title="Genjii">Genjii</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sokrates" class="mw-redirect" title="Sokrates">Sokrates</a>, <a href="/wiki/Unzan" title="Unzan">Unzan</a>) or do not have official appearances (<a href="/wiki/Youki_Konpaku" title="Youki Konpaku">Youki Konpaku</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marisa%27s_unnamed_father" title="Marisa&#39;s unnamed father">Marisa's unnamed father</a>, <a href="/wiki/Myouren_Hijiri" title="Myouren Hijiri">Myouren Hijiri</a>). However, the one with the most recurring appearances is <a href="/wiki/Rinnosuke_Morichika" title="Rinnosuke Morichika">Rinnosuke Morichika</a>, who is sometimes mocked in the fanbase over this. Another male character with a name and an official appearance is <a href="/wiki/Unshou" title="Unshou">Unshou</a>. There is also one before all of these, called <a href="/wiki/Shingyoku" class="mw-redirect" title="Shingyoku">Shingyoku</a>, but this character has three forms, so this character may not exactly be male.
</p><p>However, it has been theorised that <a href="/wiki/ZUN" title="ZUN">ZUN</a> might've had trouble designing male characters due to the number of female characters – such as statements like "it would be weird to have an old man as the last boss in Touhou" for Myouren been originally the final boss of <i><a href="/wiki/Undefined_Fantastic_Object" title="Undefined Fantastic Object">Undefined Fantastic Object</a></i> – but later confirmed that he feels that "boys get in the way of the game" and that "boys would just ruin the balance of the series."<sup id="cite_ref-ZUN.27s_AWA_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ZUN.27s_AWA-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He prefers females to fight in the games.
</p>
<dl><dt>PC-98</dt></dl>
<p>Amongst the <a href="/wiki/PC-98" title="PC-98">PC-98</a>, only some characters had a full portrait of themselves from head to feet. The PC-98 had a 16-colour limit, where one obvious feature was that most PC-98 characters had the same eye and hair colour; the only characters who have different eye and hair colour are <a href="/wiki/Elis" title="Elis">Elis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Konngara" title="Konngara">Konngara</a> and <a href="/wiki/SinGyoku" title="SinGyoku">SinGyoku</a>'s "male" form. Also, <a href="/wiki/ZUN" title="ZUN">ZUN</a> has stated that he won't go back to doing 16-colour graphics.<sup id="cite_ref-AUS3comment_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AUS3comment-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dt>Drawings</dt></dl>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:177px;"><a href="/wiki/File:ThGK_Interview.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="/images/thumb/0/09/ThGK_Interview.png/175px-ThGK_Interview.png" width="175" height="149" class="thumbimage" srcset="/images/thumb/0/09/ThGK_Interview.png/263px-ThGK_Interview.png 1.5x, /images/thumb/0/09/ThGK_Interview.png/350px-ThGK_Interview.png 2x" /></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:ThGK_Interview.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Some characters that don't appear in <i><a href="/wiki/Symposium_of_Post-mysticism" title="Symposium of Post-mysticism">Symposium of Post-mysticism</a></i> (with <a href="/wiki/ZUN" title="ZUN">ZUN</a>) by <a href="/wiki/TOKIAME" title="TOKIAME">TOKIAME</a>.</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/ZUN" title="ZUN">ZUN</a> takes on most of the drawings for the characters that appear in the Touhou games. His distinctive ways of drawing is different compared to other artists. The character portraits used in the fighting spin-off games developed in collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Twilight_Frontier" title="Twilight Frontier">Twilight Frontier</a>, however, have been drawn by <a href="/wiki/Alphes" title="Alphes">alphes</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Immaterial_and_Missing_Power" title="Immaterial and Missing Power">Immaterial and Missing Power</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Scarlet_Weather_Rhapsody" title="Scarlet Weather Rhapsody">Scarlet Weather Rhapsody</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Touhou_Hisoutensoku" title="Touhou Hisoutensoku">Touhou Hisoutensoku</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Hopeless_Masquerade" title="Hopeless Masquerade">Hopeless Masquerade</a></i>), and <a href="/wiki/Moe_Harukawa" title="Moe Harukawa">Moe Harukawa</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Urban_Legend_in_Limbo" title="Urban Legend in Limbo">Urban Legend in Limbo</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Antinomy_of_Common_Flowers" title="Antinomy of Common Flowers">Antinomy of Common Flowers</a></i>). ZUN had various artists to draw the manga and other official books, such as <a href="/wiki/Aki%E2%98%85Eda" title="Aki★Eda">Aki★Eda</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/Silent_Sinner_in_Blue" title="Silent Sinner in Blue">Silent Sinner in Blue</a></i>, and several artists in the case of <i><a href="/wiki/Perfect_Memento_in_Strict_Sense" title="Perfect Memento in Strict Sense">Perfect Memento in Strict Sense</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Makoto_Hirasaka" title="Makoto Hirasaka">Makoto Hirasaka</a> drew the <i><a href="/wiki/Touhou_Sangetsusei" title="Touhou Sangetsusei">Touhou Sangetsusei</a></i> mangas, but also drew the characters for <i><a href="/wiki/Fairy_Wars" class="mw-redirect" title="Fairy Wars">Fairy Wars</a></i> since the game is based on the manga.
</p>
